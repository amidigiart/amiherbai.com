AI System Transparency Notice

Per EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689), Article 50

System identification

  • System name: amiHerbAI Companion
  • Provider: amiHerbAI, Timișoara, Romania
  • Risk classification: Limited-risk AI system (transparency obligations apply)
  • Purpose: Informational companion for herbal medicine and phytotherapy topics

AI models used

  • Primary engine: Grok (by xAI) — general-purpose large language model
  • Secondary engine: DeepSeek (by DeepSeek) — general-purpose large language model
  • Architecture: Dual-engine anti-confabulation — both models answer independently; responses are delivered only when they agree (trigram-cosine concordance ≥ 0.45 and no numeric conflicts)

How it works

When you send a message, both AI models generate independent responses. A concordance algorithm compares them:

  • Agreement (≥0.45 cosine + no numeric conflict): Response delivered with "affirm" status
  • Disagreement: Honest refusal — "my two sources don't agree"
  • One engine down: Single response with "uncorroborated" warning
  • Both engines down: Technical error message, no guessing

Limitations

  • This system can produce incorrect information despite dual verification
  • It cannot diagnose, prescribe, or replace professional medical advice
  • Herbal information may not account for individual health conditions
  • The system's knowledge has a training data cutoff and may not reflect the latest research
  • Concordance between two models does not guarantee factual accuracy — it reduces but does not eliminate confabulation risk

Human oversight

Users must exercise their own judgment. The concordance score and engine information are displayed with every response to support informed decision-making. The system includes medical disclaimers and directs users to healthcare professionals for health concerns.
